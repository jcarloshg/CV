## Best Job Sites in USA for Software Engineers

### **Major General Platforms**

- **LinkedIn** - The top professional networking platform for mid-sized companies. Create job alerts, reach out directly to recruiters, and showcase your portfolio in the featured section
- **Indeed** - One of the largest job boards with thousands of tech listings across all levels
- **Glassdoor** - Great for job listings plus company reviews and salary transparency

### **Tech-Specific Job Boards**

- **Dice** - One of the most popular tech-focused job boards with around 2 million tech candidates. Excellent for finding skilled technical positions
- **Stack Overflow Jobs** - Perfect for developers, integrated with the developer community
- **GitHub Jobs** - Ideal if you have an active GitHub profile with open-source contributions
- **AngelList (Wellfound)** - Excellent for startup positions and early-stage companies
- **Hired** - Reverse job board where companies reach out to you after you create a profile

### **Remote & Specialized Platforms**

- **Arc.dev** - Focuses on remote opportunities for mid-level and senior developers, can land roles in as little as 14 days
- **Remote OK** - Over 800,000 monthly users, great for remote tech positions
- **FlexJobs** - Vetted remote and flexible opportunities (requires subscription)
- **We Work Remotely** - Large remote job board with many tech positions
- **TripleByte** - Engineers helping engineers find jobs with skill-based matching

### **Niche Developer Boards**

- **F6S** - Focused on startups, free job listings
- **Python Job Board** - Official Python community job board
- **Ruby on Rails Jobs** - Specialized for Rails developers
- **React Jobs** - For React specialists

## Tips for Completing USA Job Applications

### **Before You Apply**

**1. Tailor Everything**

- Customize your resume and cover letter for EACH position
- Mirror keywords from the job description in your application
- Show you understand the company and role specifically

**2. Optimize for ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems)**

- Use standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)
- Avoid tables, graphics, columns, or creative formatting
- Save as .DOCX or PDF
- Use standard section headers
- Include exact keywords from job posting
- Never put important info in headers/footers

**3. Prepare Your Materials**

- Professional resume (1-2 pages max)
- GitHub portfolio with active, clean projects
- LinkedIn profile that matches your resume
- Cover letter template you can customize
- List of references ready

### **Application Strategy**

**Never Just Apply Online**
The research shows that simply submitting an online application gives you slim chances, especially at larger companies. Instead:

**1. Find the Recruiter/Hiring Manager**

- Search on LinkedIn for "[Company Name] Software Engineering Recruiter"
- Send a connection request with a personalized message
- Mention the specific role and why you're interested
- Attach your GitHub portfolio in the message

**2. Network Strategically**

- Attend local tech meetups (check Meetup.com)
- Join online developer communities
- Contribute to open-source projects
- Engage in tech forums and discussions

**3. Follow Up**

- After 1-2 weeks, send a polite follow-up message
- If rejected, ask for feedback on how to improve
- Stay in touch with recruiters for future opportunities

### **Common Application Fields to Complete**

**Personal Information**

- Full name, email, phone number
- Location (city, state) - you can list your current location in Mexico
- LinkedIn profile URL
- GitHub/portfolio links

**Work Authorization**

- Be honest about visa status
- Many companies sponsor H-1B or offer TN visas for Mexican citizens
- Some roles are remote and don't require US presence

**Education**

- Degree, institution, graduation date
- GPA (if above 3.5)
- Relevant coursework for entry-level

**Experience Section**

- Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- Quantify achievements with numbers
- Focus on impact, not just duties
- Example: "Optimized database queries, reducing response time by 40% and improving user experience for 10,000+ daily users"

**Technical Skills**
List in order of proficiency:

- Programming languages
- Frameworks and libraries
- Tools and platforms
- Databases
- Cloud services (AWS, Azure, GCP)

**Projects**

- Include 3-5 strongest projects
- Brief description of what it does
- Technologies used
- Link to GitHub repo or live demo
- Quantify impact if possible

**Questions About Salary**

- Research market rates on Glassdoor, levels.fyi, Payscale
- For expected salary, either give a range or write "Market rate" or "Negotiable"
- Average for entry-level: $109,000
- For experienced engineers: $103,000 - $145,000+

### **Pro Tips**

1. **Apply Early** - Many companies review applications on a rolling basis
2. **Quality Over Quantity** - 10 well-researched, tailored applications beat 100 generic ones
3. **Track Everything** - Use a spreadsheet to track applications, dates, contacts, and follow-ups
4. **Prepare for Phone Screens** - Have your "elevator pitch" ready about who you are and why you're interested
5. **Practice Technical Interviews** - Use LeetCode, HackerRank, or Cracking the Coding Interview book
6. **Build Portfolio Projects** - Show real working applications, not just tutorial follow-alongs
7. **Be Patient** - Average job search takes 3-6 months, even for qualified candidates
