Aquí tienes tu respuesta estructurada:

---

**Situation:**
"When we decided to integrate generative AI to automate customer support, there was a lot of excitement but also a lot of risk. We needed a system capable of handling real user queries, but our US stakeholders were highly concerned about the AI providing incorrect information or making up company policies."

**Task:**
"My primary objective was to design a production-ready architecture using AWS Bedrock that was fast, cost-effective regarding token usage, and, most importantly, completely safe from hallucinations. At the same time, I had to ensure our non-technical stakeholders understood the limitations and the safety measures we were implementing."

**Action:**
"To tackle the hallucination problem, I completely avoided relying on the LLM's raw internal knowledge. Instead, I implemented a strict **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)** architecture.

First, I vectorized our verified company documentation. When a user asked a question, the system retrieved only the relevant context and injected it into the prompt. To manage token limits and reduce latency, I optimized the chunking strategy of our documents, ensuring we only sent the exact paragraphs needed, rather than entire manuals.

Furthermore, I applied strict system prompts and guardrails, instructing the model to reply _'I don't have that information'_ if the answer wasn't explicitly in the retrieved context.
Regarding stakeholder communication, I set up weekly syncs where I translated these technical guardrails into business value, demonstrating live how the model refused to answer out-of-domain questions."

**Result:**
"The result was a highly reliable, low-latency AI integration. We successfully automated a large portion of routine support tickets with zero reported critical hallucinations in production. More importantly, by keeping the communication transparent and managing expectations, we built absolute trust with the US leadership team regarding our AI initiatives."
